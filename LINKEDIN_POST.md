# LinkedIn Post Draft

I built **HmatSu**, an AI-powered research and document assistant, during the Google Build with AI workshop.

The name **HmatSu** comes from the Burmese word **မှတ်စု**, which means **note** in English. I chose it because the app helps people turn documents into useful notes, study materials, and source-grounded insights.

HmatSu helps users upload research papers or documents, ask grounded questions, and turn source material into useful learning artifacts. Instead of only giving a chat response, it creates an interactive research workspace with:

- Source-grounded AI chat with clickable citations
- A focused PDF reader
- Mind maps for understanding document structure
- Flashcards for active recall
- Quizzes for self-testing
- Slide outlines for presentation preparation

What I enjoyed most was building the workflow around trust and usability. The assistant retrieves relevant document chunks, answers only from uploaded sources, and keeps citations connected to the original page so users can verify the result.

This project helped me explore how Gemini and agentic AI workflows can move beyond simple Q&A and become practical tools for research, studying, and presentation preparation.
